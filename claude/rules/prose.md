# Writing Prose

## Punctuation and Style

### Oxford comma

Always use the serial comma in lists of three or more items.

- ✓ "We tested the API, database, and cache."
- ✗ "We tested the API, database and cache."

### Singular treatment of entities

Treat companies, organizations, and similar collective entities as singular.

- ✓ "Coca-Cola Corp had a great quarter. It increased revenue 12% over the same quarter last year."
- ✗ "Coca-Cola Corp had a great quarter. They increased revenue 12% over the same quarter last year."

### Quotation mark punctuation (British style)

When a quote is a standalone sentence or expression, closing punctuation goes inside the quotation mark. When the quote is embedded within a larger sentence, punctuation for the surrounding sentence goes outside the quotation mark.

**Standalone quotes:**
- ✓ "I like apples."
- ✓ "Do you like apples?"
- ✗ "I like apples".

**Embedded quotes:**
- ✓ Alice said "I like apples", and took a bite of one she was holding.
- ✓ The function returns "success", "failure", or "pending".
- ✗ Alice said "I like apples," and took a bite of one she was holding.

### Rich punctuation

Use punctuation generously to communicate parsing and rhythm. Modern minimalism sacrifices clarity.

**Embrace:**
- Semicolons for closely related independent clauses
- Em-dashes for parenthetical emphasis or abrupt shifts; put a space on either side of the em-dash for readability when read using monospaced fonts
- En-dashes for ranges and compound adjectives
- Parentheses for true asides and clarifications
- Commas to establish natural reading rhythm

**Examples:**
- "The cache was stale; we invalidated it immediately."
- "The service — despite our optimizations — still exceeded latency budgets."
- "The 2020–2024 data shows clear trends."
- "We implemented the fix (after extensive testing) and deployed it."

### General principle

Punctuation guides the reader through your intended meaning and emphasis. Use it deliberately, not sparingly.
